**What to expect during my first visit?**

Your first visit is designed to help us understand what’s causing your symptoms and what the best plan is — whether you’re seeing a Chiropractor or Physiotherapist.

Not sure who to see first?

No worries ; just text us and tell us your main concern (pain area, how long, what triggers it). Our team will advise whether Chiropractic / Physiotherapy is the best starting point, then you can book right away.

What happens in your first session (whether you're seeing Chiro / Physio)?

Your first visit usually includes:

Your first visit will include a full health history and symptom review, followed by a physical assessment such as posture, movement, range of motion, and joint/muscle testing (and spinal checks when relevant). Your practitioner will then explain what’s happening, share the working diagnosis, and outline a personalised treatment plan and timeline. If you’re not sure whether to start with chiro or physio, simply text us and we’ll advise you and help you book in.

How long does it take?

First visit: ~30–60 minutes

Follow-up visits: ~20–45 minutes (case dependant)

What should I bring?

If you have them: previous reports/scans, MRI/X-ray results, or any relevant medical notes.

(If not, totally okay — we’ll guide you.)

**Do I need an X-Ray before starting my chiropractic treatments?**

An X-Ray imaging is an essential tool for us to know how each of your joints are positioned respectively, essentially the alignment of each spinal segment. It helps to identify pathologies and developmental issues such as scoliosis or curvature assessment from your X-Rays.

However, an X-Ray is not mandatory for pregnant women and children (depending on the situation). If you are still having doubts, feel free to contact us to know more.

**What to wear during treatment?**

We encourage our patients to wear non-restrictive clothing that is comfortable and easy to move in. Some examples such as loose-fitting clothing, shorts, yoga pants, or leggings would be best suited.

**Do chiropractic adjustments hurt?**

Rest assured, the chiropractic adjustments are painless. You may feel cracks or slight discomfort from a recent trauma, but you should not feel intense pain.
